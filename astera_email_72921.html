Internal News
July 30, 2021
 
Communication & Culture


We at Astera Cancer Care hear you and support you. By now, we are all aware of the uncertainty and distress you have been experiencing during these recent months of immense change. To streamline communications we will send out emails and newsletters such as this, to inform you of new hires, job opportunities, and processes. Some updates will go to the entire organization and some will be location-based. Our leadership team meets weekly, so should you have any questions or concerns, please discuss them with your supervisor.

Our goal is to make Astera the workplace of choice for compassionate people just like you. We are committed to making Astera a place where our team members and patients feel at home. Cancer is challenging, and it takes a village to create a culture of kindness and empathy for our patients and each other.

We look forward to what the future holds as we continue to expand and provide the best possible care to our patients.

 
 
Physical Expansion of Services


There are many renovations to our existing locations, moves to new places, and a continuous evaluation of other sites to care for our patients.

If you have ever renovated your home or moved, you know how disruptive and unsettling it can be before experiencing the rewards of a comfortable, new environment. The same holds true for Astera. We need to modify our footprint to better serve our patients, families, providers, and team members.

Currently, there are renovations in most Astera locations. Hamilton was our first practice to move entirely from their long-standing space within RWJ Hamilton Hospital to our new, temporary location in the center of beautiful Robbinsville. In addition, the lease for a new, permanent Robbinsville office has been signed.

New construction delays exist due to a lack of supplies and contractors secondary to the Covid-19 pandemic. We anticipate a “move-in” date by spring/early summer 2022. Additionally, we will be moving our Somerville location to a beautiful new office in Bridgewater. That lease, too, has been signed, and due to the same barriers, has a similar anticipated “move-in” date of next spring/early summer.

 
 
Please welcome our new Director of Pharmacy-Infusion Therapy Services, Isabel Caratenuto, PharmD, BS Pharm, RPh.


Dr. Caratenuto is excited to join the Astera team to oversee infusion therapy services in all of Astera's offices.

From a young age, Dr. Caratenuto was fascinated by how medicine helped us feel better, and this transformed into her love for pharmacy with medication safety and management. Her more than 25-year career as a pharmacist gives her a diverse background in retail, home infusion, and hospital pharmacy. In addition, she covered pediatric and adult oncology, bone marrow transplant, and CAR T-cell patients.

Dr. Caratenuto hopes to use her knowledge to help physicians, advanced practice providers, and patients with their drug-related questions and concerns. She is also committed to helping patients navigate their treatment in an ever-changing dynamic oncology world with compassion and care.

 
 
Staffing

In the interim, your HR point of contact is Dana Dowdell from OneOncology. Please reach out to her with any questions at dana.dowdell@asterahealthcare.org.
